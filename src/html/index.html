Cold Spring Harbour

Cold Spring Harbour is Billy Joel's debut album, released in 1971. It showcases his early songwriting skills and features a mix of ballads and rock-influenced tracks. While not as well-known as his later albums, it's a significant part of Joel's musical journey and includes standout songs like "She's Got a Way", "Everybody Loves You Now.".

Piano Man

Piano Man is one of Billy Joel's most iconic albums, released in 1973. It marked a significant turning point in his career, solidifying his status as a singer-songwriter. The album features a collection of relatable songs about everyday life, including the title track "Piano Man" which has become a timeless classic. Notable songs include "Captain Jack,", "You're my home", "Piano man".

Streetlife Serenade

Streetlife Serenade is the third studio album by American singer-songwriter Billy Joel, released on October 11, 1974. It was the first album Joel recorded for Columbia Records. It features a mix of songs with a more upbeat tempo and a focus on his storytelling ability. Notable songs include "The Entertainer", "Streetlife Serenade", "Roberta".

52nd Street

52nd Street is the sixth studio album by American singer-songwriter Billy Joel, released on October 11, 1978. It was the first album to be released after the success of his previous album The Stranger, and features a mix of songs with a more upbeat tempo and a focus on his storytelling ability. Notable songs include "Big Shot", "Honesty", "My Life".

The Stranger

The Stranger is the 3rd studio album by American singer-songwriter Billy Joel, released on July 29, 1986. It was the last album Joel recorded for Columbia Records. The album was a commercial success, and has been certified 7x Platinum by the RIAA. The album featured seven singles, including "Modern Woman", "A Matter of Trust", "This Is the Time".
